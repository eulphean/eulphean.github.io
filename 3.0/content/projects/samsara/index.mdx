<Overview>

Samsara is a monumental LED installation that translates the ancient concept of cyclical existence into a multisensory experience. Spanning a 50-foot space with large-scale LED panels and a 12-channel spatial audio system, the piece immerses audiences in generative visuals of creation and dissolution powered by a custom GPU particle simulation.

</Overview>

<Build>

To achieve the required performance, I implemented a custom GPGPU particle simulation running entirely on the GPU. This allowed us to render over 500,000 particles at 60fps while leaving CPU headroom for audio processing and network communication.

The architecture consisted of three main components: a central control server, distributed rendering nodes for each LED panel, and a dedicated audio processing unit. All components communicated via WebSocket for sub-millisecond synchronization.

<Result>

The installation was successfully deployed at the Chicago Digital Arts Festival 2025, where it ran continuously for three weeks. We achieved stable 60fps performance across all eight LED panels while maintaining perfect audio synchronization.

The piece received critical acclaim and was featured in several digital art publications. Over 15,000 visitors experienced the installation during its run.
