<Overview>

CineFives is an AI-powered movie recommendation engine that moves beyond traditional collaborative filtering to deliver deeply personalized top-five movie lists. Through natural conversation, users describe their mood, preferences, and context, and the system responds with curated selections accompanied by meaningful explanations for each pick.

</Overview>

<Challenge>

The goal was to build a movie recommendation experience that goes beyond generic algorithms. Most recommendation systems rely on collaborative filtering, which often produces predictable, popularity-biased results. The challenge was to create a conversational AI system that truly understands individual taste, mood, and context to generate highly curated top-five movie lists.

We needed to design an architecture that could process natural language preferences, cross-reference a comprehensive film database, and deliver recommendations with meaningful explanations—all in real-time through a polished web interface.

<Build>

The system was architected around a conversational AI pipeline that combines LLM reasoning with structured film metadata. The backend processes user prompts through a multi-stage pipeline: intent classification, preference extraction, candidate retrieval from the film database, and final ranking with explanation generation.

The frontend was built as a responsive Next.js application with real-time streaming responses, allowing users to see their recommendations materialize as the AI reasons through its selections.

<Result>

CineFives launched as a public web app in 2026, quickly gaining traction among film enthusiasts. The conversational approach to recommendations proved far more engaging than traditional list-based interfaces, with users averaging over 5 sessions per visit.

The AI pipeline consistently generates nuanced, surprising recommendations that go beyond mainstream picks, earning positive reception from both casual viewers and cinephiles.
